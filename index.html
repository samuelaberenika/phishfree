Mission 01
High Frequency

Corporate Threats

Workplace phishing targets your credentials, banking details, and authority. Attackers impersonate IT, HR, payroll, and even your CEO to bypass your critical thinking under the pressure of a professional context.

Red flags to spot

  • Domains using lookalike characters - "c0mpany" not "company"
  • IT or payroll requests sent by email rather than internal systems
  • CEO or senior staff requesting urgent, confidential wire transfers
  • Unexpected scanner or document attachments from office equipment
Mission 02
High Stakes

Bank & Finance

Financial phishing creates panic to short-circuit judgement. Attackers clone your bank's branding precisely, exploit legitimate-looking subdomains, and weaponise urgency — frozen accounts, suspicious transactions, identity checks.

Red flags to spot

  • Domain is not the real one - "lloyds-bank-alert.com" not "lloydsbank.com"
  • Subdomain trick - "hsbc.phishing-site.com" puts the brand name first
  • Requesting full password, memorable word, and card number simultaneously
  • Legitimate banks always tell you to type the address - never click a link
Mission 03
Account Takeover

Social Media

Social platforms are high-value targets — account access means identity theft, audience manipulation, and contact harvesting. Attackers use deletion threats, fake reward programmes, and spoofed security alerts to force quick action.

Red flags to spot

  • Character substitution in sender domain - "inst4gram" not "instagram"
  • "24 hours until deletion" - urgency designed to stop you thinking
  • Creator rewards asking for bank account details via email link
  • Real security alerts direct you to the app - never an email link
Mission 04
Credential Harvest

Academic

Students and staff are targeted at volume through impersonation of portals, library systems, and funding bodies. Deadline pressure and financial incentives — fake bursaries, exam access — are the key weapons here.

Red flags to spot

  • UK universities always use .ac.uk - never .com or .org
  • Surprise bursaries asking for NI number and bank details by email
  • Canvas or Moodle links - always access through your institution's URL
  • IT storage warnings - real ones come from your official university domain
